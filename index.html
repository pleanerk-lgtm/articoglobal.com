The Importer of Record (IOR) is the entity legally responsible for a shipment's compliance with the destination country's import regulations. This includes correct HS code classification, duty and tax payment, import licensing, and ongoing record-keeping obligations. You need an IOR when you do not have a legal entity registered in the destination country, when local regulations prohibit a foreign company from importing directly, or when your internal team lacks the specialist knowledge to execute a compliant clearance. PGM BI acts as your IOR — we sign the forms, we hold the liability, and your goods clear without incident.
As IOR, PGM BI assumes full legal accountability for import compliance in the destination jurisdiction. This includes liability for correct duty payment, accurate declaration of goods, compliance with import restrictions and licensing requirements, and record retention obligations. We carry dedicated trade compliance insurance for this purpose. Client engagements include a clear scope-of-goods agreement — we do not take on IOR responsibility for goods that are misdescribed, prohibited, or outside the agreed product category.
